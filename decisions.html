The Second Corridor · editorial decisions

Where the record disagreed with the spec, the record won.

← the instrument methods changelog

This tracker was built against a written specification. In three places the public record contradicted what the spec said to render. Each time, the record was verified in the primary document and rendered instead — and the disagreement is published here rather than silently patched. The full decision log, including the smaller calls, lives in the repository README.

1 · CHIPS direct funding renders itemized

The spec called for a single capital-stack band labeled "single agreement, NY share not itemized." Micron's SEC Form 8-K (December 9, 2024) itemizes the finalized award: up to $6.165B in total — $4.6B for Clay Fabs 1–2, $1.5B for the Boise, Idaho fab, and up to $65M for workforce — executed as two funding agreements under one finalized award. Shipping the spec's label would have shipped a false statement; the itemized, citable version renders in Fig 03.

2 · The "~$12M federal workforce" figure is omitted

The spec carried a ~$12M federal workforce figure. No such figure could be located in any public document (searched: NSF, NIST, Commerce, the White House, DOL, Natcast). The figures that exist in the record are $40M in the April 2024 preliminary terms (superseded) and up to $65M in the final agreements. The $65M renders, cited to the 8-K; the unverifiable number does not render at all.

3 · "88 NY semiconductor companies" is date-qualified

The spec's "88 NY semiconductor companies" is, per the governor's April 2022 announcement, a count of establishments as of that date; the state's current count is 156+ companies (Empire State Development). Both figures render in section 05, each carrying its date; the talent lattice's supplier node uses the current 156+.

Why publish this

A tracker that adjudicates conflicts with its own specification — and says so — is auditable in a way an essay is not. The same discipline applies forward: future conflicts between a source document and anything this site renders are corrected toward the record and logged on the corrections log or, for routine agency revisions, the data changelog.
