The Second Corridor tracks New York State's semiconductor buildout (2022–2045) from public records and public statistical data. Its operating rule is printed on the masthead: every number cited, estimates labeled — and the rule is enforced mechanically, not editorially.

The citation gate

Every figure on the site references a key in a citation registry. A figure whose key is absent from the registry does not render — the gate is enforced in code, and a unit test sweeps every authored figure on every build to confirm its key resolves to a verified source. Each entry in the registry was located in a primary public source and then adversarially re-verified against the live document before first render.

Where the record contradicted the site's own working spec, the record won, and the disagreement is published on the editorial decisions page.

Plan, derived, measured

Part One (sections 01–05) draws the published plan: announcements, funding agreements, the environmental record. Part Two (sections 06–11) is measurement: data fetched from BLS, Census, NSF, USAspending, and NYISO. Every plate is stamped RECORD, DERIVED, MEASURED, or CONTEXT so the two are never confused.

  • Derived series are interpolations, not forecasts. The Fig 02 curves run linearly between cited anchors and are labeled INTERPOLATED BETWEEN CITED ANCHORS — NOT A FORECAST. The exact rules are published in the repository README and implemented in src/data.js.
  • Suppression is information. Where an agency withholds a cell (BLS confidentiality, survey scope), the site renders the suppression — hatched, labeled, with the reason — never a zero, never an imputed value.
  • Takeaway lines are arithmetic on already-cited figures or a direct restatement of the record, computed from the data at render time so a refresh can never strand a typed number. The site states adjacency, never cause.
  • Vintages render from provenance. Each data file embeds a provenance block (source, URL, vintage, retrieval date, license); every date printed on a plate comes from there, never from markup.

Refresh cadence

The data refreshes itself: a weekly full refresh (Mondays) re-fetches all eight sources, validates each against a JSON Schema and cross-field invariants, and opens a reviewed pull request when the published record actually changed; a daily check watches USAspending for the one record this tracker most expects — a Micron CHIPS award appearing. Refreshes that only append new periods (no value revisions) merge automatically after 72 hours; anything that revises a published value waits for review. Every change is logged in the data changelog, the prior state is archived in the repository, and source URLs are snapshotted to the Internet Archive.

The built site is self-contained: all data ships as committed JSON with the page, and the QA battery proves the deployed site makes zero requests to external services.

Contact & corrections

Corrections are welcome — Alex@ozarkintelligence.com or GitHub issues.

Policy: errors are corrected in place as soon as they are verified; every correction is logged below with the date, what was wrong, and what changed; a material error — one that changes what a figure says — is additionally noted on the affected plate for 90 days. Routine data revisions by the source agencies are not corrections; they flow through the changelog.

Data dictionary

The terms the plates use, defined once. Plate captions link here.

NAICS 3344
"Semiconductor and other electronic component manufacturing" in the North American Industry Classification System. The employment series count every NAICS-3344 employer in a county — not Micron headcount.
QCEW
BLS Quarterly Census of Employment and Wages — an administrative census of employers covered by unemployment insurance. A county-industry cell flagged non-disclosable (code N) renders as suppressed; a county-industry row absent from the file means zero covered establishments and renders as a measured zero. The two are different facts and render differently.
OEWS
BLS Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics — a survey estimate of wages by occupation and metro. Occupations the survey cannot estimate locally are "not published": absence of an estimate, not absence of workers.
LODES (JT00)
Census LEHD Origin–Destination Employment Statistics. JT00 counts jobs, not workers — a person holding two jobs counts twice — so the site says "commutes," not "commuters." All-industry: OD files carry no industry breakout, and the site does not imply one.
IPEDS · CIP
The federal postsecondary data system; completions are counted by Classification of Instructional Programs code. Completions lag roughly two years; the site re-keys all years to NCES survey years. AY 2021–22 means the academic year ending 2022.
ACS B15003
American Community Survey 5-year table for educational attainment, population 25+. Five-year estimates trade currency for county-level reliability.
FAIN · CFDA
Federal Award Identification Number, and the Catalog of Federal Domestic Assistance program number (CHIPS Incentives = 11.037). An award without a FAIN on USAspending has not been published to the federal spending record — that absence is itself a datapoint this site tracks daily.
Announced · obligated · outlaid
Three different facts about federal money: a press release, a binding commitment recorded on USAspending, and cash actually disbursed. CHIPS direct funding disburses against milestones, so obligated ≠ behind-schedule.
Suppressed · not published · measured zero
The site's three absence states, each with its reason printed: agency confidentiality (QCEW S), survey scope (OEWS), and a true zero in the administrative record. None of the three is ever rendered as a value.
Vintage · retrieved
The period the data describes vs. the date this site fetched it. Both print on every measured plate.
NYISO Zone C · MW · MGD
The grid operator's Central NY load zone (the megafab's zone); megawatts; million gallons per day (water figures, from the project's Final Environmental Impact Statement).
schemaVersion
Each published JSON carries a schema version; structural changes increment it. See the data contract.

Limitations

What the record doesn't say (yet) — maintained on the instrument at "the honest margins," restated here for permalink:

  1. The construction ramp-down after 2041 is illustrative — no cited figure describes how the workforce winds down.
  2. The talent lattice's ribbon widths are illustrative; only its structure — who feeds whom — is cited.
  3. No NYISO document publishes a full-buildout megawatt figure; the cited interconnection requests (2026 Gold Book, Table IV-7) stop at Fab 2.
  4. Commerce has published no award record on USAspending for the Micron funding agreements — the federal ledger renders the absence.
  5. QCEW counts every NAICS-3344 employer in a county, not Micron headcount — the plan series and the measured series answer different questions.
  6. OEWS publishes no semiconductor-technician wage for either corridor metro — the occupation the buildout most needs is the one the survey can't yet see here.

Stable anchors

These URLs are a documented, stable surface — inbound deep links will not be broken:

  • /#s01–/#s12, /#record-gaps, /#sources — section anchors.
  • /#src-N — individual source rows. Footnote numbers are append-only: new sources are added at the end of the list, never renumbered.
  • /#y=YYYY — the instrument at a given year; &view=section and &p=flows carry the full instrument state.
  • /f/01–/f/12 — per-figure share URLs (redirect to the section, carry per-figure preview cards, accept the same state hash).
  • /data/*.json — the published data files, per the data contract.
  • ?nointro — skips the opening animation.
